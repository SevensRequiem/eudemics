# Rights

What is inherent versus what is granted, and the limits of both.

## Default Competency

Most adults are presumed competent. Rights are not earned; they are the default. The right to exist, to be treated with dignity, to receive care, and to be safe are unconditional. These do not vary with intelligence, competency tier, or any other measure.

Participation rights (see [[decision-making/participation]]) may vary based on demonstrated competency, but basic human rights never do.

## Competency and Self-Direction

Genuine inability to think for oneself - severe cognitive impairment, acute psychosis, advanced dementia - triggers a guardianship process. This is a high bar, and the assessment is rigorous.

### Dual Guardianship

When someone is genuinely unfit for self-direction:
- Both the state AND loved ones participate in decision-making
- Loved ones provide personal context and emotional care
- The state provides institutional safeguards and medical expertise
- Neither acts alone

Why dual? Loved ones can be uninformed, self-interested, or actively harmful. The state can be impersonal or bureaucratic. The combination prevents the worst failure modes of either alone.

### What Is NOT Grounds for Guardianship

- Making bad choices
- Holding unusual beliefs
- Being neurodivergent (autistic, ADHD, etc.)
- Non-conformity or unconventional lifestyle
- Disagreeing with family or officials

The standard is functional capacity, not normality. See [[health/mental]] for rigorous assessment criteria.

### Anti-Abuse Safeguards

The 1950s demonstrated what happens when "mentally unwell" is weaponized: families and states locked away inconvenient people. The model prevents this:

- **Multi-evaluator requirement** - no single person (family member, doctor, official) can trigger involuntary assessment. Requires multiple independent assessments.
- **Evidence-documented** - every evaluation is recorded, RFC 3161 timestamped, and auditable. The assessment trail itself is subject to review.
- **Time-bounded** - every guardianship has an expiration date. Renewal requires fresh evaluation.
- **Neurodivergence explicitly excluded** - autism, ADHD, unusual thinking patterns, and non-conformity are never grounds for competency challenges.
- **Adversarial review** - the person being assessed can request independent counter-evaluation at state expense from any node they choose.

## Age Progression

Rights and system participation are not a hard cutoff at age 18 but a gradual progression:

- Children begin in the educational pipeline (see [[knowledge/education]])
- Internet access is age-gated (see [[knowledge/information-flow]])
- Competency assessment engages gradually through adolescence
- Parental authority decreases as the child's demonstrated competency increases
- The system protects children from parents who would game assessments or restrict development

Evidence shows genuine genius can emerge as early as age 9. The system accommodates this rather than holding capable children back for arbitrary age reasons.

## Death

On death:
- Data pack enters cold storage in the global data pool (see [[security/resilience]])
- Token distribution follows the person's will
- Family override available for disputes
- All records are preserved permanently
