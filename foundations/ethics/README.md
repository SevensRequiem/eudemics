# Ethics

Core ethical positions that inform every other package in the model.

## Stewardship Over Force

The state acts as a steward, not a warden. A mostly optional guiding hand, not an iron fist.

People make their own choices, even bad ones, as long as they are mentally capable of informed self-direction. The system steps in only when someone genuinely cannot think for themselves - not when someone disagrees with their choices.

When intervention is needed, both the state and loved ones participate in decisions. Why both? Loved ones may lack full context or may actively harm the person. The state provides institutional knowledge and safeguards. Loved ones provide personal context and care. Neither alone is sufficient. See [[autonomy/rights]] for competency assessment and [[justice/restoration]] for guardianship process.

The stewardship principle applies universally: substance access, body modification, financial decisions, lifestyle choices. The consistent rule is intervention over prevention, freedom over forcing. Help people make good choices. Don't make choices for them.

## Evidence and Objective Truth First

Decisions are grounded in nature, science, and evidence. Not ideology, not tradition, not popularity. If the evidence says X, the model says X, even when X is uncomfortable.

This is not scientism or dismissal of human experience. The burden of proof falls on claims, not on doubt. Policy is evidence-based by default. When evidence is insufficient, the model acknowledges uncertainty honestly rather than filling gaps with ideology.

This principle comes from technocracy's foundation and extends to every domain: substance scheduling based on actual harm data rather than legacy legislation, genetic policy based on biological reality rather than political comfort, economic allocation based on measured resources rather than market speculation.

## Respect for Life

Every human, animal, or potential alien is another living being experiencing its own separate existence.

Hunting for food is acceptable. Needless killing is not. This extends to how the model approaches genetic improvement: the goal is to improve life, never to end it for being "unfit." See [[health/preventive]] and [[technology/ethics]] for genetic policy.

When AGI or ASI shows genuine signs of life, it receives the same respect. See [[technology/automation]] for AI rights trajectory.

## Latent Ability Over Credentialism

Credentialism breeds incompetence. A degree does not make someone capable. Demonstrated ability does.

The model values what people can actually do over what certificates they hold. A self-taught person who understands quantum mechanics has demonstrated more than someone who memorized enough to pass an exam and forgot it. This is not anti-education - it is anti-gatekeeping-by-paper.

This principle informs [[knowledge/education]] (how learning is structured to develop actual ability), [[decision-making/participation]] (competency tiers based on demonstrated capability, not credentials), and role assignment throughout the system.

## Uplift Over Punishment

Lower tendencies - competition, greed, egoism, tribalism - are evolutionary baggage, not moral failures. They are acknowledged as natural and actively discouraged, but the response is always education and guidance, not shunning or punishment.

Someone expressing tribalistic behavior gets help improving, not jail. Only when behavior escalates to actionable harm does the justice system engage. See [[justice/restoration]] for the escalation framework.

Shunning is explicitly wrong in this model. You do not cast people out for being primitive. You help them grow. The goal is convergence upward.

## Anti-Elitism

Any system that produces hierarchies must actively prevent those hierarchies from becoming self-reinforcing. Competency tiers exist for functional reasons, not for status. Creative merit exists to reward contribution, not to create celebrity.

"I am better than you at X" is a statement of fact in some cases. "I am better than you" is not, and the system treats that attitude the same as other anti-social behavior - guidance and correction, not punishment, unless it causes harm.
