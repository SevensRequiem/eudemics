# Human Nature

What humans evolved for, what we did not evolve for, and what that means for system design.

## Evolutionary Baggage

Humans evolved in small tribal groups. Competition for resources, in-group/out-group dynamics, status hierarchies, aggression, greed - these are not bugs in human character, they are features of a survival strategy that worked for 200,000 years and is now maladapted to modern civilization.

The model does not pretend these tendencies can be eliminated overnight. It designs systems that account for them, channel them productively where possible, and discourage them where they cause harm. See [[foundations/ethics]] for the uplift-over-punishment approach.

Post-industrial society demands behaviors humans did not evolve for: sitting still for 8 hours, abstract knowledge work, delayed gratification over decades, cooperation with thousands of strangers. Forced labor in this context is not natural and the model's approach to work reflects this. See [[resource-systems/production]] for work week reduction and AGI transition.

## Neurodivergence as Variation

Autism, ADHD, dyslexia, schizotypal thinking, and other neurodivergent profiles are variations in human cognition, not defects. An autistic person processes the world differently. That is not incompetence.

The competency system (see [[decision-making/participation]]) evaluates reasoning ability, meta-cognition, and demonstrated capability - not conformity to neurotypical patterns. The mental health assessment system (see [[health/mental]]) explicitly excludes neurodivergence from grounds for competency challenges.

Being different is not being broken. The system must never conflate the two.

## Racial Biology

Race is a biological reality shaped by tens of thousands of years of geographically separated evolution. Populations that evolved in North Africa, East Asia, Northern Europe, and South America developed in different environments under different selection pressures.

The honest position: there may be measurable average differences between populations in various traits. This is a scientific question to be studied without political interference, not a social hierarchy to be enforced.

If research finds genuine disparities, the response is medical and genetic improvement, not discrimination. "This population has higher rates of X condition" means fix X, not rank populations. See [[technology/ethics]] for genetic improvement policy and [[health/preventive]] for implementation.

The goal is convergence upward for all populations, not hierarchy. See [[community/belonging]] for how the model handles race socially.

## Hatred and Lower Tendencies

Hatred that causes harm (incitement, harassment, targeted campaigns) is criminal. Internal feelings and low-level tribalism are natural but discouraged.

Competition, greed, and egoism are looked down upon but never shunned. The system never encourages these behaviors - it just encourages getting better. The response to someone acting on primitive impulses is always the same: help them improve, don't push them away.

See [[justice/restoration]] for the escalation framework from guidance to containment.
