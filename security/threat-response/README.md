# Threat Response

How the system identifies, prepares for, and responds to threats.

## Chemical Weapon Substances

Substances where the potency-to-amount ratio makes weaponization trivial remain prohibited. Fentanyl and carfentanil are not drugs in any practical sense when micrograms are lethal and dispersal is trivial. They are chemical weapons. See [[autonomy/liberty]] for the substance tier system.

## Cyber Attacks

Nation-state and criminal cyber attacks are routed to nodes with appropriate cybersecurity resources. The system does not expect every node to defend against sophisticated digital threats. Nodes with tech offices and cybersecurity competency handle these.

Response coordination happens through the automated node signaling system (see [[security/resilience]]). Affected nodes signal, capable nodes respond.

## Kinetic Threats

Ballistic missiles, nuclear threats, and asteroid impacts:
- Nodes with relevant defense infrastructure activate
- Cities maintain active defense systems appropriate to their threat environment
- Global coordination for existential threats (asteroid impact, nuclear exchange)
- Preparation is ongoing, not reactive

## Alien Contact

No evidence of alien life exists. Limited preparation for contact is still practical engineering. The model does not spend heavily on this, but it does not spend zero either. First-contact protocols, communication frameworks, and quarantine procedures exist in outline form.

## Disease Outbreak

Per-node health infrastructure is the first line of defense. Neighboring nodes assist. Global coordination activates for pandemics.

The node structure is inherently resistant to pandemic spread: nodes can isolate if needed without collapsing the whole system. The survival stores and redundant communication ensure isolated nodes remain functional.

## Climate and Environmental Threats

Sea level rise, extreme weather, and climate change are ongoing threats, not hypotheticals:
- Coastal nodes prepare for flooding and sea level rise
- All nodes factor climate projections into long-term planning
- Environmental monitoring is continuous (see [[ecology/sustainability]])
- The resource system adjusts dynamically as environmental conditions change
