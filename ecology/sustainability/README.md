# Sustainability

Environmental carrying capacity as a hard constraint on everything else.

## Environmental Capacity as Economic Input

Environmental carrying capacity is not a preference. It is a hard constraint fed directly into the resource accounting system (see [[resource-systems/accounting]]). Prices reflect environmental cost. Resource limits respect environmental limits.

Even if supply is unlimited (asteroid mining), environmental impact at point of use constrains what the system allows. Unlimited raw materials do not mean unlimited production if the oceans cannot absorb the pollution.

## Measurement and Enforcement

- Per-node measurement of local environmental capacity
- Neighbor nodes cross-verify (prevents gaming)
- Global enforcement of environmental constraints
- Any node can choose to study any other node's environment. Transparency is default.
- Environmental data feeds directly into the resource pricing system

## Climate Restoration

Climate change mitigation and restoration is a state priority, not an optional initiative. Environmental monitoring is continuous. Long-term planning accounts for climate projections. See [[security/threat-response]] for climate-related emergency preparedness.

## Local Environment Preservation

Resource extraction and development respect local ecosystems. A region with fragile ecosystems has tighter resource limits than a robust one. The accounting system handles this automatically through environment-weighted pricing.
