# Participation

Who participates in decisions, and how competency is evaluated.

## Competency-Stratified Participation

Not everyone's opinion carries equal weight on every topic. This is not elitism - it is functional design. A pilot flies the plane, not a vote of the passengers.

Someone who is genuinely uninformed about a topic should not be making decisions about it or claiming authority they do not have. But they should never be prevented from learning about it. The system aggressively invests in making everyone MORE competent. See [[knowledge/education]].

## Domain-Specific Competency

Competency is per-domain, not a single score. Someone brilliant at engineering might be ignorant about medicine. You get participation rights in areas where you demonstrate understanding.

This prevents the failure mode of "general intelligence tests" becoming class markers. Your physics competency does not affect your right to participate in agricultural policy.

## Holistic Mind Evaluation

IQ alone is inadequate. The assessment captures:

- **Reasoning patterns** - can you take novel information and draw valid conclusions? Not what you know, but how you think.
- **Meta-cognition** - can you evaluate your own thinking? Do you know what you do not know?
- **Memory and integration** - can you hold multiple concepts and synthesize them?
- **Domain transfer** - can you apply principles from one field to another?
- **Epistemic humility** - do you update beliefs when presented with evidence?

This is designed so a self-taught person who actually understands a field can demonstrate it without credentials. And a credential-holder who cannot reason outside memorized material gets appropriately tiered. See [[foundations/ethics]] for the latent-ability-over-credentialism principle.

## Anti-Gaming Measures

The assessment must resist coaching, memorization, and credential farming:

- Adaptive testing that changes based on responses (cannot prep for specific questions)
- Practical demonstrations, not multiple choice
- Evaluators rotate and are themselves competency-verified
- Periodic re-assessment (your tier is not permanent)
- Anomaly detection for coaching patterns
- The system is explicitly designed to be ungameable - unable to be passed through test prep alone

## What Assessment Does NOT Evaluate

- Social skills, personality, or conformity
- Specific knowledge (domain-gating is separate from general competency)
- Neurotype - being autistic, ADHD, schizotypal is not incompetence
- Cultural background or language fluency
- Speed of response (some people think slowly and deeply)
