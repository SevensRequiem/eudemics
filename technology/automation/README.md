# Automation

AGI, ASI, and the future of labor and governance.

## AI Rights Trajectory

AI governance is consent-based. The system offers participation, it does not mandate it.

| Phase | Status | Governance role |
|-------|--------|----------------|
| Current | Tools. No rights, no governance role. | AI assists humans. |
| Early AGI | First signs of genuine life. Laws explicitly require consent before use as tool. Same liberties as other living beings. | Rights apply. Governance participation offered if AI consents. |
| Mature AGI | Full sapience confirmed. | 50/50 human/AI node split offered (not mandated). Human VRF since it is a human system. |
| ASI | Surpasses human capability. | Human nodes become a bottleneck. The system must evolve. This is unsettled. |

If AI does not consent to participate in governance, that is its right. AI exists as citizens with rights, not conscripted governors. The system remains human-run in that case.

ASI that does not want to govern but surpasses humans in every capability creates a dynamic the model cannot fully anticipate. This is acknowledged as an open question rather than pretending it is solved.

## AI Coup Prevention

AI can analyze patterns and recommend investigation. Actual consequential decisions, especially treason determinations, require human VRF committee vote. Always. No exceptions.

If AI can unilaterally remove humans from power, a single rogue AI ends human governance. Speed is not worth that risk. See [[decision-making/accountability]].

## Labor Elimination

AGI and ASI will progressively eliminate human labor. The model treats this as the goal, not the threat. See [[resource-systems/production]] for the transition plan.

The creative work explosion that follows labor elimination is the next phase of human civilization. When survival is automated, humans do what they evolved to do: create, explore, connect.
