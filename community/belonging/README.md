# Belonging

How people relate to each other across differences.

## Race

Race is a biological reality shaped by geographically separated evolution. The model studies it honestly, without political interference, and responds to findings with medicine, not hierarchy.

If research finds disparities between populations, the response is CRISPR and medical improvement, not discrimination or denial. "This population has higher rates of X condition" means fix X. The goal is convergence upward for all.

See [[foundations/human-nature]] for the scientific position and [[technology/ethics]] for genetic improvement policy.

## Anti-Shunning

The model never casts people out. Someone expressing primitive behavior (tribalism, prejudice, exclusion) gets guidance and education, not exile. Shunning reinforces the very tribalism the model works against.

The only exception: behaviors that cause direct harm escalate through the justice system. See [[justice/restoration]].

## Social Bonds

The model supports community formation organically. Nodes are geographic communities by default. Shared challenges, shared infrastructure, and shared governance create natural bonds.

What the model does not do: force community, mandate social participation, or engineer belonging. People connect naturally when material needs are met and social structures are not hostile.
