# Culture

How cultural and religious identity coexists with the model.

## Heritage Preservation

Each node documents and preserves its local cultural heritage. The global replicated data pool (see [[security/resilience]]) includes cultural archives from every node worldwide.

Languages, traditions, art, cuisine, spiritual practices: all preserved as human history. Cross-node cultural exchange is actively encouraged. Culture is studied, celebrated, and shared.

## Religion and Governance

No religious, ideological, or cultural group exceeds 30-40% of any governing body. This includes atheism, which is also unproven and gets the same treatment.

More than 50% of any group inevitably biases decisions toward their worldview, even unintentionally. The cap forces coalition and compromise. Every perspective has representation. No single perspective dominates.

## Cultural Exclusion

Cultures that generate in-group/out-group dynamics ("better than thou") are acknowledged as natural but actively discouraged. The model does not suppress culture. It does not let cultural identity become a basis for exclusion or superiority.

You can celebrate your heritage. You cannot use it to gatekeep access, status, or belonging. See [[community/belonging]] for how this applies to race and social groups.
