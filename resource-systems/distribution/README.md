# Distribution

How resources reach people.

## Universal Basic Income

Every citizen receives UBI tokens. The baseline is generous: enough to buy a car, furnish a home, eat well, access entertainment, save for wants. This is not subsistence. It is a life worth living.

Not equal wealth, but no poverty. The floor is high. You can accumulate tokens above the baseline through work, creativity, or other contribution. Having more tokens than someone else is fine.

## Resource Footprint Caps

Token accumulation is unlimited. Physical resource hoarding is not.

Someone owning 30 cars and driving one is wasteful. The model constrains material accumulation, not financial accumulation.

- Token purchases of physical goods carry a resource footprint
- Your total active resource footprint has a dynamic cap
- Want a new car? Trade in or dispose of the old one, or the footprint cost increases steeply
- This incentivizes efficient use without limiting financial freedom

Resource footprint caps are dynamic, tied to the accounting system's real-time supply and environmental data. See [[resource-systems/accounting]].

## Creative Merit Tokens

Novel creativity earns tokens above the normal UBI allowance. This nurtures independent thought and rewards what humans do that machines cannot (at least pre-ASI).

### Evaluation

- Anonymous assessment by peers within that creative niche plus some randomly selected evaluators
- Niche peers assess technical quality and novelty
- Random evaluators prevent echo chambers and insider favoritism
- Anonymous both ways: evaluators do not know the creator, creators do not know the evaluators

### Anti-Elitism Measures

Creative tokens must not become status currency:
- **Merit decays** - creative tokens reflect recent contribution, not lifetime achievement. Resting on laurels normalizes the bonus.
- **No public leaderboards** - the system knows who earned what for allocation, but there is no public ranking.
- **Anti-narcissism** - "I earned more creative tokens than you" is treated the same as other superiority behavior. The system rewards creation, not creators. See [[foundations/ethics]] for the anti-elitism principle.

### State Recognition

Genuine inventions or creative works that help the whole earn more than just tokens. The inventor gets permanent credit, bonus tokens, and state celebration: feasts, recognition events, extra resources. Making the person feel genuinely accomplished, for an evidence-based duration (not too short to feel hollow, not too long to become worship).

## Intellectual Property

- Creator holds copyright with a TTL (time-to-live). You own your work but not forever.
- Anti-copyright-trolling: holding creative copyright for 500 years and blocking derivatives is anti-system.
- MIT-license-style approach: original ownership credit required, but others can build on it.
- Patents are anti-system. You do not own ideas. Genuine inventions earn inventor credit (permanent) and bonus tokens, not monopoly rights.
