# Production

How work is organized, and how the model handles the transition to post-labor society.

## Work Week Reduction

Technocracy's 16-hour work week is the starting point. As automation increases, the work week decreases. The trajectory is toward zero mandatory labor.

This is not aspirational. It is a design parameter. The UBI system (see [[resource-systems/distribution]]) must function whether 90% of people work or 5% do.

## AGI/ASI Transition

AGI and ASI will eventually eliminate most human labor. This is not a threat - it is the goal. Humans did not evolve for post-industrial forced labor (see [[foundations/human-nature]]). Freeing them from it is progress.

The transition plan:
- As automation replaces job categories, those workers transition to other work or creative pursuits
- UBI absorbs displaced workers without economic disruption
- The work week shrinks continuously as automation capabilities grow
- Eventually, labor is entirely optional

See [[technology/automation]] for the timeline and governance of AI systems.

## Creative Work Explosion

When material needs are met and labor is optional, humans create. Art, music, writing, invention, research, exploration. This is what humans do when survival pressure is removed.

The resource system values creative output through the creative merit token system (see [[resource-systems/distribution]]). Creative work is not just tolerated in a post-labor economy - it is the primary form of human contribution.
