# Ownership

What can be owned, and the limits of ownership.

## Property

People buy and own property with tokens. There is no reason someone should not be able to own land and housing.

But limits exist: if everyone spreads out maximally, no land remains for network infrastructure (datacenters, logistics, manufacturing, public spaces, natural preserves).

- Land ownership caps per person, dynamically adjusted based on population density and infrastructure needs
- You can own property. You cannot hoard land.
- Development reservation: a percentage of available land in each node is reserved for network use

## Material Goods

Similar to land: you can buy anything you want. The resource footprint system (see [[resource-systems/distribution]]) prevents hoarding of physical resources.

Owning a car is normal. Owning 30 cars is wasteful and the footprint cost makes it impractical. The system does not ban it, it just makes resource inefficiency expensive.

## What Cannot Be Owned

- Ideas and natural phenomena (patents are anti-system, see [[resource-systems/distribution]])
- Public waterways, airspace, and critical natural resources
- Other people (obviously)
- Data about other people (your data is yours, see [[autonomy/privacy]])
