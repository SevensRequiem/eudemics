# Information Flow

How information moves through the system, who can contribute where, and how media is regulated.

## Tiered Internet

Everyone can READ everything. Learning is unrestricted. Always. A curious person at any tier can read physics papers, medical research, engineering docs. The system never restricts access to knowledge.

CONTRIBUTING to professional and technical spaces requires verified competency in that domain. If you want to post in the physics forum, you demonstrate physics understanding. If you want to publish medical guidance, you demonstrate medical competency.

This is not about credentials. A self-taught expert who demonstrates genuine understanding gets the same access as someone with formal education. See [[decision-making/participation]] for how competency is assessed holistically (reasoning patterns, meta-cognition, not just IQ or degrees).

### Age-Gated Access

Internet access progresses with age and demonstrated capability:
- 1-8: learning content only
- 9-13: expanded learning, broader access
- 12+: less restricted, exploitation-filtered
- 16-18: near-full access, competency tiers engage

See [[knowledge/education]] for the full pipeline.

### Bot and Fraud Mitigation

The KYC-linked identity system (see [[autonomy/privacy]]) means every internet user is a verified human (or verified AI, once AI rights apply). Sites never see your actual identity, but the ISP-held hash enables accountability.

This eliminates: LLM bot farms, sockpuppets, coordinated inauthentic behavior, most spam, and many fraud vectors. The internet becomes a space for genuine human (and eventually AI) interaction.

## Media Regulation

### Mandated Categorization

All media must be categorized: news, opinion, entertainment, advertisement, educational, etc. Disguising opinion as news is illegal. The audience always knows what kind of content they are consuming.

### Corporation Power Limits

Corporate lobbying is illegal. Corporations operate within the system's rules; they do not write them. Meta lobbying for rules that benefit Meta is exactly the corruption this model prevents.

Invasive advertising gets strict limits. Free market for products, not free-for-all for attention capture. Advertising that uses manipulative psychological techniques faces consequences.

### Lower-Tier Accessibility

People at lower competency tiers are more vulnerable to manipulation. The system addresses this:
- Higher-tier people are assigned to create simplified explanations of complex media and events
- AI and LLMs provide real-time "explain this to me" for any content
- These explanations are optional, not forced. The guiding hand, not the iron fist.

### Political Messaging

Does not exist in this system. There are no political parties, no campaigns, no political advertising. Governance is git-based and VRF-based. Policy is debated on evidence. See [[decision-making/consensus]].

### Foreign Disinformation

Network egress is unrestricted. A high-tier citizen can browse foreign internet freely. Network ingress is gatekept: foreign content entering the system goes through source verification and mandatory categorization.

This is not censorship of ideas. It is quality control on provenance. You can read whatever you want. The system just makes sure you know where it came from and whether the source is verified.
