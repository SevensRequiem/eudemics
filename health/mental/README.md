# Mental Health

Competency assessment, neurodivergence, and preventing the weaponization of mental health labels.

## Competency Assessment Criteria

The standard for declaring someone unfit for self-direction is high and specific:

- **Behavioral, not ideological** - "they believe weird things" is not a criterion. "They cannot feed themselves or recognize danger" is.
- **Functional capacity** - can the person make decisions and act on them? Not whether others agree with those decisions.
- **Stability over time** - a single bad day does not trigger assessment. Persistent inability to function does.

## Multi-Evaluator Requirement

No single person can trigger involuntary competency assessment:
- Requires multiple independent evaluations from different assessors
- Assessors are competency-verified themselves
- Assessors rotate to prevent collusion
- Family members cannot unilaterally commit someone
- State officials cannot unilaterally commit someone

## Neurodivergence Exclusion

Explicitly and permanently excluded from competency challenges:
- Autism spectrum conditions
- ADHD
- Dyslexia
- Unusual thinking patterns
- Non-conformity
- Eccentric behavior
- Unpopular opinions

Being different is not being unwell. The system never conflates divergent cognition with impaired cognition. See [[foundations/human-nature]].

## Anti-Abuse Safeguards

Every evaluation is:
- Recorded and RFC 3161 timestamped
- Auditable by the person, their advocates, or any node
- Time-bounded (guardianship expires, renewal requires fresh evaluation)
- Subject to adversarial review (the person can request counter-evaluation from any node, at state expense)

Smear campaigns, family disputes, and political disagreements cannot be weaponized through the mental health system. The evidence standard is clinical and functional, not social or political.

## Treatment Approach

Mental health treatment follows the stewardship model:
- Available and free for anyone who wants it
- Not forced on people who are functional but unconventional
- Forced only when functional capacity is genuinely absent (the high bar described above)
- Goal is always restoration of capacity, not permanent control
